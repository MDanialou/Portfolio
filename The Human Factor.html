The Human Factor

Module Objective

This module offers an interdisciplinary perspective, involving Psychology, and Computer Science, to equip students with the skills and knowledge to develop pragmatic methods to meet human factor cyber security challenges. It helps students develop an understanding of the security issues and risks associated with how people interact with computing systems and help them develop the knowledge and skills required to manage and mitigate cyber risks in socio-technical systems.

Module Outcome

The outcome of this module is to: - Review of the concepts and principles of human behaviour within the context of cyber security. - Development of an in-depth understanding of the elements of risk in a socio-technical system. - Critical examination of effective strategies to promote and manage the human factor in cyber security. - Critical evaluation of the social and ethical considerations in developing and applying usable security.

Unit 1:

Human Factors and Cyber Security

Main areas - Exploration of human behaviour and its implications in designing computing systems within the context of cyber security. - Discussion on human capabilities and limitations and their impact on usable security. - Discussion on the concept of insider threat and accidental insider and their effect on security.

Unit 2: Fitting the Task to the Human

Main areas

- Coverage of the four key principles to designing security to fit to humans. - Identification of specific user groups and appropriate security design solution for such groups. - Identification of available techniques and technologies to facilitate the design.

Unit 3: Mental Models and Accessibility Issues

Main areas

- Exploration of the use of mental models in identifying user perceptions and security preferences. - Coverage of the use of mental models as an approach to designing usable security. - Review of the contextual design process and how they are applied to usable security. - Discussion on the concept of accessibility and their implications.

Unit 4: Usable Security Design

Main areas

- Explore the various techniques to consider when planning a usable design solution. - Discuss the strengths and weakness of the techniques and their application contexts. - Apply the contextual design approach to a case study.

Unit 5: Ethical, Societal and Organisational Issues

Main areas.

- Discuss cultural, organisational issues and their impact on security. - Discuss the framework to develop a positive, organisational security culture. - Cover the use of mental model as security risk communication tool. - Discuss the legal and ethical requirements and implication when developing usable security. - Reflect on the current and future research in the various areas of usable security and human factors in cyber security.

Unit 6: Mental Models and Risk Communication

Main areas.

- Employ mental models to understand user behaviours. - Learn how to use mental models to address specific human factor issues. - Explore the use of metaphors as a technique to communicate risks.
